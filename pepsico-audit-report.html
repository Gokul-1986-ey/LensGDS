Section 02
Target Company Overview
PepsiCo, Inc. — Global food, snack and beverage leader with a uniquely integrated portfolio

PepsiCo is one of the world's largest food and beverage companies, operating across more than 200 countries and territories. Unlike pure-play beverage companies, PepsiCo's defining competitive advantage is its dual-business model — a market-leading snack business (Frito-Lay) combined with a global beverage portfolio — giving it unparalleled retailer shelf space, distribution density, and cross-category consumer reach. The company operates seven reportable segments: Frito-Lay North America (FLNA), Quaker Foods North America, PepsiCo Beverages North America (PBNA), Latin America, Europe, Africa/Middle East/South Asia (AMESA), and Asia Pacific/Australia/NZ/China (APAC).

Section 03
Industry Overview & Value Chain
Global food & beverage landscape, mega-trends, and PepsiCo's end-to-end supply chain mapping

The global food and beverage industry is a ~$8 trillion market. Packaged food & beverage (PepsiCo's core) is ~$2.5 trillion annually. The sector is characterized by intense brand competition, rising private-label pressure, commodity cost volatility (corn, potatoes, PET resin, aluminum), and an accelerating shift toward healthier, functional, and sustainable products. Digital commerce, direct-to-consumer channels, and AI-powered demand sensing are reshaping how F&B companies plan and execute their supply chains.

Coca-Cola is PepsiCo's most direct and formidable competitor in beverages. Its asset-light franchise bottling model structurally delivers superior gross margins (61.6% vs. PepsiCo's 54.1%) because Coca-Cola manufactures concentrate and licenses production to franchise bottlers — offloading manufacturing capex and inventory risk. This is PepsiCo's most significant structural disadvantage. However, PepsiCo's snack business (which Coca-Cola completely lacks) provides revenue diversification and higher DSD route utilization.

Kraft Heinz competes with PepsiCo primarily in the packaged food aisle — condiments, cheese, meats, and cereals. Its 2025 results are a cautionary tale: $9.3B in impairment charges signal deep brand equity erosion, and organic revenue is declining 3.4%. For PepsiCo, Kraft Heinz represents a competitive validation that brand investment, innovation, and health-aligned portfolio transformation are non-negotiable — not optional — for long-term category leadership.

Section 09
Strategic Recommendations
Top 10 prioritized initiatives — ranked by impact vs. effort, for CSCO / COO / CXO audience

Quick Wins (0–30 Days)

Rec #1
Accelerate 20% SKU Cull Program
Finalize the rationalization of ~240 underperforming SKUs across Frito-Lay and beverages. Reduces changeovers, lowers forecast error by ~15%, and frees $300–500M in inventory working capital.
High Impact
Rec #2
Extend Supplier Payment Terms (DPO +10 Days)
Renegotiate payment terms from ~55 to ~65 days with top 200 strategic suppliers — matching Nestlé's benchmark. At PepsiCo's ~$43B COGS, each 10 days DPO extension = ~$1.2B in freed cash.
High Impact
Rec #3
AR Automation to Reduce DSO
Deploy e-invoicing and dynamic discounting tools for top 500 retail customers. Targeting DSO reduction from ~38 to ~30 days releases ~$2.0B in receivables at PepsiCo's revenue scale.
High Impact

Mid-Term (3–6 Months)

Rec #4
Scale "One North America" DSD Integration
Accelerate the Texas pilot into a national rollout of combined snack + beverage distribution. Combining 2 DSD networks into 1 could reduce last-mile cost by 15–20%, worth $500M–$1B annually.
High Impact
Rec #5
AI-Powered Demand Sensing Deployment
Leverage the newly deployed Frito-Lay SAP platform to integrate ML demand sensing. Target forecast accuracy improvement from ~78% to ~90%, reducing safety stock by 20% and DIO from ~36 to ~28 days.
High Impact
Rec #6
Inventory Optimization Across Beverage Network
Apply Frito-Lay's inventory management learnings to the beverage segment. PBNA carries disproportionate finished goods buffers. Target: DIO reduction from ~36 to ~28 days, freeing ~$1.5B in working capital.
Medium Impact

Strategic Bets (6–18 Months)

Rec #7
Build Integrated Supply Chain Control Tower
Unify visibility across 290+ manufacturing plants, DSD routes, and retail execution into a real-time control tower. Closes 2-point digital maturity gap vs. Coca-Cola's best-in-class AI supply chain.
High Impact
Rec #8
Accelerate "Better-For-You" Portfolio Transformation
Aggressively invest in Gatorade Zero, Quaker, Bubly, and functional snack innovation. BFY is the fastest-growing shelf segment. Directly counters the health-trend risk threatening CSD and traditional salty snack volumes.
High Impact
Rec #9
Dual-Source Agricultural Commodities (Corn, Potato)
Implement dual-sourcing for Frito-Lay's top 5 agricultural inputs across geographically diversified supplier pools. Reduces weather/tariff disruption risk and improves negotiating leverage on ~$8B+ in raw material spend.
Medium Impact
Rec #10
APAC & Emerging Market Supply Chain Buildout
Invest in local manufacturing and DSD infrastructure in India, Southeast Asia, and Sub-Saharan Africa. These markets represent $5B+ in addressable revenue headroom. Nestlé's footprint shows the long-term margin potential of early-mover advantage.
High Impact
